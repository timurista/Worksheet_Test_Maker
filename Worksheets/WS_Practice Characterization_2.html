    13. Types of Characters Section

  13.  The dynamic character is:
      a) a character who changes usually over the course of the novel
      b) a character who does not change over time; his or her personality does not transform or evolve.
      c) a character who may not always be admirable (e.g. an anti-hero); nevertheless s/he must command involvement on the part of the reader, or better yet, empathy.
      d) a character or a group of characters which stand in opposition to the main character.
  14.  The minor character is:
      a) a person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone or something; an adversary
      b) a character who does not change over time; his or her personality does not transform or evolve.
      c) a character of less importance than the major character, but who is necessary for the story to develop.
      d) a character who responds to events and seems to change their personality over time
  15.  The protagonist is:
      a) a character who responds to events and seems to change their personality over time
      b) a character or group of character which serve to complement the major characters and help move the plot events forward.
      c) a person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone or something; an adversary
      d) a character who is the central person in a story, and is often referred to as the story's main character
  16.  The antagonist is:
      a) a character who changes usually over the course of the novel
      b) a character who does not change over time; his or her personality does not transform or evolve.
      c) a character who is the central person in a story, and is often referred to as the story's main character
      d) the character who is keeping the main character from getting what they want.
  17.  The static character is:
      a) a character who is the central person in a story, and is often referred to as the story's main character
      b) a character who changes usually over the course of the novel
      c) a character who does not change over time; his or her personality does not transform or evolve.
      d) a character or group of character which serve to complement the major characters and help move the plot events forward.
